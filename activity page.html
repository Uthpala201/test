...4.Hiking...



. Hiking in Sri Lanka is something that most tourists have fantasized about at some point. The country's name alone conjures up images of lush, green rainforest, rolling mountains, tropical wildlife, and turquoise water. The best hiking in Sri Lanka exemplifies this.


. Lonely Planet named the country the "best country in the world" for travelers to visit in 2019. They praised its "rich and accessible wildlife," as well as the surf scene, culture, and people's friendliness.





...HIKE THROUGH THE KNUCKLES MOUNTAIN RANGE...



. A lot of hiking in Sri Lanka takes place on well-traveled paths and routes. In the Knuckles mountain range, no. This is the route we take on our own adventure in Sri Lanka, taking a couple of nights to get through the Knuckles. Ruvi, an experienced local guide, told us that "even Sri Lankans don't really know the trail that we're hiking on," and joked that when he showed the photos to other guides upon his return from the route, they all wanted to go visit.



. The trail takes you deep into the rainforest and into the rugged wilderness of the Knuckles Range. The range got its name from its resemblance to a clenched fist. Consider yourself immersed in majestic mountains that rise up to 2000 meters. The scenery is dotted with massive cascading waterfalls.


. You pass through small villages and spend the night wild camping in the middle of nowhere. You'll almost certainly see buffalos, and if you're lucky, you might also see boars, toque macaques, and purple-faced monkeys. If you're really lucky, you might even see elephants and leopards in the jungle. Though the chances of running into them while hiking with their backpacks are slim.
